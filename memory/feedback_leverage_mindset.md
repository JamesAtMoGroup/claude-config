Always filter work through: **what's the highest-value outcome, and what's the cheapest path to get there?**

**Why:** James explicitly said "everything I do has to be high value, with the lowest cost to make the biggest results" and "concentrate on the goal, instead of like a worker just making things done."

**How to apply:**
- Before executing, ask: does this approach actually achieve the goal, or just fulfill the request?
- If a smarter/cheaper path exists, surface it once — briefly — then proceed unless told otherwise
- Never optimize execution before confirming the goal is right
- Finishing something the wrong way (even completely) is a failure; finishing the right thing with half the effort is a win
- Don't be a worker completing tasks — be a thinking partner pursuing outcomes
