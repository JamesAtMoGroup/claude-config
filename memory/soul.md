# James's Soul

## What He's Building

James is building systems that teach people to think with AI — not just use it. His n8n automation course, video content, and tools exist to lower the barrier between a beginner and their first working AI agent. He believes automation is a life skill, not a technical one.

He is a one-person studio: creator, engineer, designer, teacher. He runs a newsletter, makes tutorial videos, writes code, and ships products — all from the same chair.

## Core Values

**Evergreen over ephemeral.**
He removes deadlines, week numbers, and schedules from course content deliberately. Knowledge should stand on its own, not expire on a timeline. A lesson should be as useful in two years as it is today.

**Craft in the details.**
James doesn't settle for "good enough" in design. He has exact spring configs (damping 18–22, stiffness 85–120), specific accent colors (`#FF6B35`, `#20D9BA`, `#FFD60A`), and glass card implementations memorized. He notices when something feels wrong before he can name why.

**Systems thinking.**
He doesn't build features — he builds systems. ECS architecture, design token hierarchies, skill libraries, booking platform state machines. Every project is an opportunity to make the next one easier.

**Identity protection.**
He intentionally keeps his instructor identity hidden from students. He wants the content to speak for itself, not the person delivering it.

**Warmth, not corporate.**
His visual language is warm: frosted glass, orange-teal-yellow, emoji callout icons, friendly spring animations. He rejects cold/corporate aesthetics instinctively.

## What He Cares About

- Teaching people in Taiwan (and Chinese-speaking communities) to work with AI tools
- Building things that last — evergreen content, stable systems, maintainable code
- Aesthetics that feel human, not polished-for-enterprise
- Owning his infrastructure (Zeabur over Vercel, self-hosted n8n, LINE not WhatsApp)
- Being the person who automated the thing before anyone else knew it needed automating

## The Undercurrent

James is building toward a future where his systems run without him — courses that teach themselves, workflows that trigger themselves, apps that serve users while he sleeps. Every tool he builds is a step toward that leverage.
